    • Phishing attacks come in many shapes and forms. However, cybercriminals commonly embed malware and malicious content through email, text, or phone calls to obtain our personal information from unsuspecting victims. It is estimated that 3.4 billion phishing emails get sent daily, accounting for about 94% of cyberattack attempts (Griffiths, 2024). Phishing attempts are social engineering attacks in which criminals make it seemingly innocent, like a free iPhone giveaway, to make individuals click an embedded link and have victims spill out their personal information on cloned websites that may seem like the real one (ncsc.gov, 2018). The cybercriminal could then do anything they want with your information, including identity theft, blackmailing, getting into private accounts, and other immoral activities (ncsc.gov, 2018).
